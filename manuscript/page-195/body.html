The ligature may be either of twine, silk, or elastic cord. We have ourselves operated by this method in the successful removal of growths of very considerable size. So long as the upper portion of the cord, which retains its healthy structure, can be reached, the application of the ligature is attended with no difficulty, the manipulations required being similar to those which attend the removal of all growths by the process of ligation.
