Swelling Of The Scrotal Region.

This, as we have seen, is an almost necessary consequence of the operation, the swelling making its appearance a short time after the alteration is accomplished. It usually first affects the parts immediately around the edges of the wound, and spreads forwards and upwards in such a manner that the entire scrotum and sheath become the seat of it. It is somewhat warm, tense, and slightly painful.
